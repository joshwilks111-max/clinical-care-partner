Hand-built HTML (AI image gen was blocked by OpenAI org verification). This is the better tool anyway — the decision is structural (interaction + layout), which static images can't show. Both frames are live and scrollable.

A · Clinical Chatbot

conversation thread · message bubbles · ChatGPT-style
Open A full-screen ↗

✅ The case FOR chatbot

  • Familiar — the reviewer needs zero learning curve; meets Heidi's note-driven mental model.
  • Honest about the medium — it IS an LLM doing turns; chat maps onto that naturally.
  • Maximum reuse — AI Elements Conversation/Message/Tool/InlineCitation give it to you nearly free.
  • Gestures at the 12-month vision — a care-partner you converse with during a live consult.

❌ The case AGAINST

  • Looks like "Heidi Evidence + a calculator" — the exact differentiation gap the review closed.
  • The safety spine scrolls away — dose trace, provenance, completeness aren't persistent.
  • The two-turn STOP reads as "did it freeze?" in a thread.
  • The open composer invites free-text the trust boundary doesn't want.

B · Structured Care-Partner Console

two-panel workspace · stepped Turn 1 → Turn 2 · not a chat
Open B full-screen ↗

✅ The case FOR structured

  • Makes the judgment→execution architecture visible — the thing being graded.
  • Safety spine is persistent + provenance-labeled (DET vs LLM badges) — scannable at a glance.
  • The two-turn STOP reads as obvious ("your turn" buttons are the next step).
  • Structure enforces the deterministic, injection-resistant demo rails the design wants.

❌ The case AGAINST

  • Less familiar — a custom workspace, not the ChatGPT pattern everyone knows.
  • More layout to hand-build (though AI Elements leaf components still slot in).
  • Further from the "conversational live-consult" 12-month vision.
